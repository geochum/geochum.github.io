About Me

Welcome! I’m George Chumbipuma, currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Computational & Applied Mathematics at Rice University. My academic journey has been fueled by a passion for exploring complex mathematical problems and applying computational methods to solve real-world challenges.

My research primarily focuses on numerical methods, optimization techniques, and high-performance computing. I am particularly interested in the application of these methods to problems in mathematical physics and engineering. My goal is to contribute to advancements in these fields through rigorous research and collaboration.

I have also been involved in education throughout my academic career, serving as a math educator and mentor. Teaching has allowed me to refine my communication skills and develop a deep understanding of how to convey complex concepts in an accessible way. This experience has been invaluable in both my academic and professional development.

Outside of my academic work, I run a tutoring service, Pumatics, where I offer personalized tutoring in math, science, computer science, and test preparation. You can learn more about this venture on the Tutoring page.
